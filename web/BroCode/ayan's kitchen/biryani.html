Biryani

ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 4-5 whole green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish



instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.

  2. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and the soaked, drained rice. Cook the rice until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain and set aside.

  3. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Sauté until the spices release their aroma.

  4. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

  5. Add chopped tomatoes, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.

  6. Add chicken pieces and cook until they are no longer pink. Stir in yogurt and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the gravy thickens.

  7. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).

  8. In a large ovenproof dish, layer half of the partially cooked rice, followed by the chicken masala, and then the remaining rice.

  9. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook.

  10. Garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves before serving. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.

Enjoy your homemade Chicken Biryani!
